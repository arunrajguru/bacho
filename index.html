Process

A five-step pipeline.
Milliseconds to safety.

Step 01
Text Preprocessing
Emoji normalization, currency symbol detection, sentence segmentation, and tokenization prepare the raw message for deep analysis.
Step 02
Feature Extraction
12 feature vectors are computed: payment intent, urgency density, free-then-pay contradiction, promise plausibility, behavioral pressure, and more.
Step 03
Pattern Matching
Over 50 regular expressions and lexical rules match specific scam phrases, informal payment channels, and suspicious link patterns in real-time.
Step 04
Risk Scoring
A weighted classifier combines all signals into a 0–100 risk score. Threshold logic determines scam category: HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW risk.
Step 05
Explainable Report
Each flagged phrase is highlighted in context. A human-readable recommendation tells you exactly what to do next — specific to the threat type detected.
Threat Intelligence

Four vectors.
One detector.

बाcho identifies all common patterns used to target students in India's internship and job market.

💰 Type 01
Hidden Payment Trap
Offers a "free internship" then requests ₹300–₹1500 as a registration, training material, or certificate processing fee. The fee is the product — no real internship exists.
⚡ Type 02
Urgency Manipulation
"Only 15 seats left." "Apply within 24 hours." "Last date: tomorrow." Artificial scarcity is designed to prevent you from verifying the offer before committing.
🏆 Type 03
Unrealistic Promises
Guaranteed placement, letters of recommendation, certificates from Fortune 500 companies — all designed to inflate perceived value before extracting a fee.
📲 Type 04
Informal Channel Pressure
Using WhatsApp, personal UPI IDs, and bit.ly links instead of corporate channels is a hallmark of scam operations — legitimate companies never recruit this way.
